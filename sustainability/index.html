Sustainability

Sustainability

For Workspace Studio, the most sustainable piece of office furniture is the one that stays in service for decades. Sustainability runs through how products are made, how long they stay in use, how easily the corporate interior adapts as the organisation changes, and how the operation itself is certified. The logic is simple: quality and longevity keep furniture out of the waste stream and cut the carbon embedded in replacing it.

Sustainable production

Workspace Studio sources by default from manufacturers that hold environmental certifications and run circular-economy programmes. Recycled and recyclable content is specified across furniture, flooring and architectural products, from carpet tiles with recycled-content backing to seating built for disassembly and material recovery.

Choosing suppliers who are accountable for their own production footprint keeps that accountability inside every project specification.

Durability is sustainability

Durability is the first sustainability decision, and it is made at specification. A chair kept in service for over a decade carries far less lifetime impact than one replaced every few years. Herman Miller seating, the iconic Aeron included, carries a 12-year manufacturer warranty covering all-day use, which sets the expected service life across a decade and more of daily wear. The same durability lowers lifecycle cost, so the sustainable choice and the financially sound one tend to be the same piece.

Design that lasts

Furniture that stays visually relevant stays in use. The office furniture industry has its own version of fast fashion: low-cost pieces that look dated within a few seasons and get thrown out.

Workspace Studio specifies from the MillerKnoll catalogue of design classics, pieces that have held their place in production and in demand for decades. The Eames Lounge Chair has been made continuously since 1956, the Aeron since 1994. Design that keeps its relevance is design that does not need replacing.

Adaptability and certification

Partition systems, furniture, flooring and architectural products are specified as modular and demountable, so the office can be reconfigured as teams grow and change, with minimal waste and demolition.

The building fabric is calibrated for the full lease term on the same logic. ISO 14001 environmental management and an EcoVadis sustainability rating anchor the operation, alongside project-level certifications such as LEED, BREEAM and WELL where the client's targets call for them.
