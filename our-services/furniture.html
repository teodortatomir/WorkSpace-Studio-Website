MillerKnoll Certified Dealer

MillerKnoll is a global collective of design brands built on the foundation of two icons of modernism: Herman Miller and Knoll. Together with Geiger, NaughtOne, HAY, Maharam, Maars, DatesWeiser, Muuto and other heritage brands, the group represents over a century of design research at the intersection of contract furniture, architectural systems and textiles.

The design heritage spans foundational modernist work by Charles and Ray Eames, Eero Saarinen, Harry Bertoia, Florence Knoll, Ludwig Mies van der Rohe, George Nelson and Marcel Breuer, alongside continuous research in ergonomics, sustainable manufacturing and workplace performance led by MillerKnoll Research.

Workspace Studio is the MillerKnoll Certified Dealer in Romania. The certification covers direct supply from MillerKnoll factories, manufacturer pricing, original product warranty, in-house specification expertise and access to MillerKnoll Research that informs how the products evolve.

Direct partnerships beyond MillerKnoll

Beyond MillerKnoll, Workspace Studio holds direct supply partnerships with hundreds local furniture manufacturers and international suppliers across Europe, North America and Asia.

The portfolio covers everything a modern corporate office needs: ergonomic task seating, height-adjustable workstations, executive offices, conference and training rooms, lounge and breakout furniture, café and dining, acoustic pods, storage and custom architectural elements.

Direct supply removes intermediary margins and shortens lead times. Brand selection on each project is informed by the brief, budget range, ergonomic requirements and aesthetic direction, with Workspace Studio selecting the manufacturer that fits the project best.

Workspaces Types

A modern corporate office is no longer one continuous open floor of identical desks. It works as a portfolio of distinct settings, each calibrated to a different mode of work. Workspace Studio specifies and supplies furniture across the full range.

Individual workstations

Where focused work happens, ergonomics drives everything. The right combination of an adjustable task chair, a height-adjustable desk, a properly positioned monitor and adequate task lighting reduces musculoskeletal strain across an 8-hour workday and supports concentration on cognitively demanding tasks.

The MillerKnoll seating range (Aeron, Embody, Mirra 2 and the other ergonomic chairs) anchors the offer, calibrated for body type and seating posture rather than treated as a single ergonomic standard.

Meeting rooms

The variable has shifted from "how many people fit at the table" to how quickly the room can reconfigure between a 4-person huddle, a 12-person workshop, a hybrid call with five people on screen, and a confidential one-on-one. Tables on castors, modular seating, integrated AV, acoustic separation and writable surfaces support the full range.

Flexibility is now a baseline requirement of any meeting space.

Cafeterias and social spaces

The cafeteria, the coffee bar and the social table do work that no scheduled meeting can replicate. Spontaneous conversations between people from different teams, casual mentoring moments, the cross-functional context that travels faster over coffee than over Slack, plus the relationship-building that converts colleagues into a culture.

Comfortable furniture, food-service-adjacent layouts and acoustic separation from focused work zones make these spaces functional in practice.

Connecting spaces

Soft-seating clusters, standing-height tables near coffee points, lounge zones between teams. These spaces support collaboration that happens without a calendar invite: a quick problem-solving conversation, an impromptu brief, a five-minute alignment that prevents a thirty-minute meeting next week, or a casual check-in between sprints. Modular soft seating and writable surfaces turn dead circulation space into live collaboration infrastructure.

Office pods

Open-plan offices solve for collaboration density but create a problem for everything that needs intimacy: a confidential call, a focused 90-minute task, a private one-on-one, a video call where being overheard is unacceptable. Acoustic phone booths and meeting pods (Framery and other manufacturers) provide privacy on demand, without the overhead of permanently building enclosed rooms. Intimacy when you need it, density when you don't.
