General Contracting

Exceptional execution when the design is set.

Construction works, mechanical and electrical installations, furniture supply and architectural solutions under one site team. The same discipline we apply to our own Design & Build projects.

General Contracting is Workspace Studio's execution model for office projects where the design is already set, supplied by your architect, by a landlord designer, by a global corporate design team or by Workspace Studio under a separate design contract. Our in-house site team takes over from the design handover point and delivers the build under a single contract.

Scope of work

As an experienced general contractor, Workspace Studio oversees all interior construction elements, including:

Partitions, ceilings and finishes

Flooring and carpeting

Mechanical, electrical and plumbing (MEP) coordination

Health & Safety management

Continuous on-site supervision

Quality assurance and clear reporting

This ensures controlled progress, predictable timelines and high-quality results.

Project Steps in General Contracting

How a General Contracting project moves from design handover to completion

1.

Design review and constructability check. We start with a working session on the design handed over by the client or external design team. Our architects and engineers review drawings for constructability, MEP coordination, technical gaps and procurement feasibility. Issues raised at this stage save weeks on site later.

2.

Procurement and pre-construction. Bill of quantities issued, framework agreements activated with manufacturers and subcontractors, long-lead items ordered. Direct manufacturer relationships on furniture, flooring, partitions and acoustic products give Workspace Studio shorter lead times than typical resellers.

3.

Site mobilisation. Site setup, HSE briefing, temporary works, demolition where needed. The site manager assigned at this stage stays on the project through to handover.

4.

Construction and MEP execution. Partitions, ceilings, flooring, MEP integration, joinery and finishes are run by Workspace Studio's in-house trades and selected subcontractors, coordinated under one site manager, on one schedule, with daily quality control. Critical-path work stays in-house.

5.

Furniture installation and commissioning. Furniture delivered just-in-time from the warehouse, installed by the same logistics team that handles Day-2 service. MEP systems commissioned, fire safety tested, snagging completed.

6.

Handover and Day-2 service. Documentation pack delivered, keys handed over, warranty period activated. The same team stays on for warranty handling, reconfigurations and maintenance through Workspace Studio's Logistics & Facilities Services.

Quality & site management

Built like our own designs

Most fit-out companies execute. But Workspace Studio has experience in turn-key projects and sees the larger picture. Site teams that have spent 15+ years correcting design errors on their own projects bring a different eye to executing someone else's design.

In-house trades on critical paths. Partitions, MEP integration, joinery and finishes are handled by Workspace Studio's directly employed teams. Subcontractors cover specialised scope (specialist flooring, glazing, signage) but the critical-path trades stay in-house. One chain of accountability, one quality standard.

MEP coordination as a discipline, not an afterthought. Mechanical, electrical and sanitary installations account for most of the technical risk on an office fit-out. Our engineers run MEP coordination as a dedicated workstream from pre-construction through commissioning, with daily site reviews and weekly client reporting.

HSE record. Zero notifiable incidents across the corporate fit-out portfolio. ISO 45001 certified for occupational health and safety, alongside ISO 9001 (quality management) and ISO 14001 (environmental management).

Quality control built into daily rhythm. Quality checks happen daily on site, not as a punch-list exercise at the end. Snagging at handover is final verification, not the first time someone looks closely.

The same procurement discipline, in-house trade coordination and quality control standards apply as in our Design & Build projects. The difference is contractual scope, not execution standard.
