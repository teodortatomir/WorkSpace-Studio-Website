Ergonomics

Inclusive ergonomics: design that works for everyone

Inclusive ergonomics designs for the real range of bodies, sensory profiles, cultural expectations and life stages from the start.

Real workforces include different bodies, sensory profiles, cultural expectations and life stages.

Standard ergonomic guidance was built around an "average" worker who does not exist. Real workforces include different bodies, different sensory profiles, different cultural expectations and different life stages. Inclusive ergonomics designs for that range from the start, so the office works for the people who actually use it.

Neurodiversity.

An estimated 15-20% of people are neurodivergent, with different needs around sensory input, focus, predictability and stimulation. Inclusive design offers a spectrum of settings, from low-stimulation quiet rooms to enclosed focus pods, so people can match the environment to how their attention works.

Accessibility.

Step-free routes, accessible workstations, clear sightlines, tactile and visual wayfinding and adjustable furniture make the office usable for people with mobility, vision, hearing or other physical differences. Accessibility belongs in the brief from day one.

Families and new parents.

Returning to the office is particularly hard for people with young children. Inclusive design eases that transition with dedicated lactation and wellness rooms for new mothers, family rooms for the days when childcare falls through, parent-friendly quiet spaces and the practical provisions that make a working day workable under pressure. Small interventions, large effect on whether parents stay and return.

Cultural differences.

Expectations around privacy, hierarchy, prayer or quiet space and the balance between individual and collective work vary across cultures. International workforces need a range of settings rather than a single imposed norm.

Age differences.

A workforce spanning four generations has a wide range of needs around lighting levels, acoustic clarity, screen legibility and physical comfort. Designing for the older end of the range tends to improve the experience for everyone.
