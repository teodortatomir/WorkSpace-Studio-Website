Social ergonomics: connection by design

Physical ergonomics looks after the body. Social ergonomics looks after how people connect, collaborate and work alongside each other.

Connection at work is engineered deliberately into the floor plan.

Physical ergonomics looks after the body. Social ergonomics looks after how people connect, collaborate and work alongside each other, and connection at work is something you engineer deliberately into the floor plan.

An office filled with open desks and a token coffee corner does not automatically produce collaboration. Just as often it produces noise, distraction, interruptions and the kind of low-grade friction that damages focused work, while the conversations that actually matter happen somewhere else or never happen at all. The difference between a floor that supports connection and one that undermines it comes down to deliberate design.

Good social ergonomics puts the right settings in the right places. Cafés and informal zones where cross-team conversations form by chance, the casual encounters that strengthen the looser professional relationships research links to innovation and knowledge flow. Connecting spaces for quick, unscheduled collaboration. Meeting rooms and focused zones positioned so that collaboration does not bleed into concentration. The aim is a floor where the right interactions become more likely and the wrong interruptions become rarer.

Designed well, social ergonomics raises collaboration and focus at the same time. Designed carelessly, an office trades one against the other and tends to lose both.
