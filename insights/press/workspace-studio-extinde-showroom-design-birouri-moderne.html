Press release / October 2023

Workspace Studio expands its showroom to reflect current trends in modern office design, centered on employees.

The Sky Tower Bucharest showroom now covers 850 sqm, following an investment of more than EUR 300,000.

Press releaseBack to press releases

Studies on the future of offices and work point unanimously to flexibility, varied design, and a focus on employee needs. In this context, Workspace Studio, specialized in high-quality office design and fit-outs and a Certified Dealer of the MillerKnoll group in Romania, completed the expansion of its showroom located on the 18th floor of Sky Tower Bucharest, to reflect current trends in modern office design. The showroom now has a total area of 850 sqm, compared with 580 sqm before the expansion. The fit-out investment amounted to more than EUR 300,000.

Workspace Studio's investment is based on the premise that spaces fitted out to high-quality standards, oriented toward employees and with a design that supports, inspires, and stimulates creativity, are a source of competitive advantage and are becoming a necessity recognized by more and more organizations in Romania.

In the showroom, clients can explore furniture design brands such as Herman Miller, Knoll, HAY, Muuto, NaughtOne, GEIGER, and Maharam, sustainable acoustic solutions, as well as the latest trends in ergonomic furniture and office fit-out solutions, with inspiration for modern, personalized, and innovative work areas.

We emphasize and present in the showroom these personalized and innovative work environments because all the turnkey interior fit-out solutions we propose are personalized for each company, with an emphasis, of course, on ergonomics, sustainability, and especially design centered on people, their activities, and their interactions. The objective of every project is to create spectacular offices where people want to be, offices that inspire them and stimulate their creativity and desire to create added value in the company, says Horatiu Didea, Managing Partner Workspace Studio.

The new role of offices: facilitator of connections and mirror of organizational culture

As hybrid and remote work stabilize as a norm, organizational culture becomes an essential component of work strategies. A recent Gartner survey* shows that business leaders believe culture is the greatest concern when developing work policies. The company space must reflect this culture. That is why every refurbishment project begins with a discussion about the organization's culture and how the workspace can support it.

When asked why they would choose to come to the office, 74% of participants in a global survey** covering 10,000 office employees highlighted camaraderie, community, and connection as the main reasons. This shows that the modern workplace must be ready to facilitate rich interpersonal connections between colleagues, whether these occur in physical meetings or in virtual environments. At the same time, 15% of employees go to the office to focus better on what they need to do, while 11% go for visibility in front of management and career opportunities.

These results from the Future Forum 2023 study, conducted by MillerKnoll and a group of companies from diverse industries, are confirmed by the latest Gensler Global Workplace Survey Comparison 2023***. According to this report, employees value the office for focusing on their work, meeting colleagues, and having access to critical tools and people.

The office is a central point in the strategy of bringing teams to the office. It becomes a space where operational efficiency blends with creativity, a place where employees can come together and collaborate productively. Through this investment in the showroom, we aim to bring forward modern and functional solutions for companies that value the positive impact a well-thought-out work environment has on employee productivity and satisfaction, adds Horatiu Didea.

The mix of spaces and experiences plays a fundamental role in making the office more attractive

Most employees who work from the office less than 80% of a typical workweek say*** they would be willing to come to the office more often to experience an ideal combination of experiences. These were described as a variety of spaces inspired by restaurant, hotel, and residential design, focus areas such as libraries, team collaboration areas, and informal spaces for socializing and relaxation.

Recent offices fitted out by Workspace Studio in Romania stand out through the same mix of solutions that contribute to wellbeing and support the current needs of employees. Generous collaboration and relaxation areas, with varied colors and styles, can occupy almost 70% of office space. The rest is allocated to individual work formats, distinguished by appropriate ergonomic furniture and tools.

Flexibility of work and flexibility of fit-out

The Future Forum 2023 study indicates that flexibility, defined mainly by where and when people work, is not only desired by employees, but also has an immediate impact on retention. While 65% of employees want a hybrid model between office and other spaces, 20% prefer full-time office work, and 15% prefer full-time remote work. At the same time, 75% of respondents who want more schedule flexibility are inclined to look for a new job in the next year.

The need for flexibility is also reflected in the current design philosophy. Office fit-outs must allow workspace personalization according to the needs of each moment, whether this means adaptations at the individual level or changes to team spaces on an hourly or even daily basis.

Change by the minute means that individuals or teams can adjust and adapt personal work points or team spaces in real time to respond to immediate needs. Change by the hour refers to situations where more significant modifications are needed and spaces can be rearranged within a few hours to meet the specific requirements of an activity or event. Change by the day means that organizations can plan and execute larger-scale reconfigurations to support changes in strategy or workflow, with minimal impact on daily operations.

Planning changes across all three levels - minute, hour, and day - not only strengthens organizational flexibility and resilience, but also helps maximize the value of the workplace investment.
