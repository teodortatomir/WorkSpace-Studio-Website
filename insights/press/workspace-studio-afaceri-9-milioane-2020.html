Press release / Bucharest, February 2020

Workspace Studio reached EUR 9 million in revenue in 2020.

Investments of EUR 350,000 during the pandemic supported the showroom expansion and e-retail launch.

Press releaseBack to press releases

Workspace Studio, a company specialized in workplace solutions focused on ergonomics and Herman Miller's only accredited partner in Romania, closed 2020 with revenue of EUR 9 million. The financial results were influenced by the pandemic, but the context created the ideal setting for accelerated expansion into retail.

Workspace Studio adapted quickly to the new reality of the labor market. While the second quarter and part of the third quarter of 2020 were strongly affected by market uncertainty and real-estate changes, in the fourth quarter the company managed to recover by offering new solutions adapted to the context, both for offices that continued to operate and through its expansion toward B2C.

Workspace Studio revenue in 2020 reached EUR 9 million, with B2C representing 7% of the total, up from 3% in 2019. The end of the year maintained positive momentum after restrictions were lifted, both in B2C and in B2B projects, with the last quarter generating almost 30% of total revenue.

Together with Allspace Interiors, our subsidiary specialized in fit-out works, partitions and flooring, we exceeded EUR 10 million in a year full of uncertainty and without any large project. It is another sign that, despite a certain decline in the office market, clients are looking for higher-quality products and solutions and reliable partners able to provide high-level professional services, says Horatiu Didea, Managing Partner of Workspace Studio.

The most sought-after products in 2020 remained Herman Miller ergonomic chairs, with accelerated momentum driven by remote work and employers' focus on investments in ergonomics and quality. They were followed by acoustic booths that provide privacy and separation, as well as air purification solutions purchased by companies that kept offices open and adopted a hybrid home-and-office work model.

Ergonomics, at home and at the office

Companies focused on employee comfort and wellbeing also directed budgets toward supporting ergonomic home-office setups. The average investment supported by employers was EUR 1,500 per employee. At the same time, companies that started office refurbishment projects turned more than ever toward high-quality products, with the average investment in a workstation increasing by around 20% compared with 2019 and exceeding EUR 1,800 per workstation.

The future belongs to companies whose employees will be productive in a hybrid work system, both at the office and remotely. 2021, the year following the restrictions caused by the pandemic, is shaping up as a year in which spaces become airier and develop multifunctional areas. Offices will be used more for teamwork, knowledge transfer, education, integration and decision-making than for individual processing work, which will remain predominantly remote, adds Horatiu Didea, Managing Partner Workspace Studio.

2020 investment projects: EUR 350,000 in an expanded showroom and online store

The growth in demand for ergonomic furniture in the residential segment in the first half of 2020 led Workspace Studio to move toward retail. The company invested EUR 100,000 in launching an online store that keeps in stock the products most requested by this customer segment, namely Herman Miller ergonomic chairs. Over the next two years, this segment is estimated to generate revenue of more than EUR 1 million.

Most employees will continue to work remotely in 2021 as well, with labor market changes that will continue in the coming years. Therefore, the need to provide comfortable workspaces that support productivity at home is continuously growing, says Horatiu Didea.

In 2020, Workspace Studio also invested EUR 250,000 in expanding its own showroom, which has become a benchmark for 2021 office market trends. The total area occupied by the showroom in the Sky Tower building in northern Bucharest increased by 45%, reaching 550 square meters.

We expect our market segment, workplace design oriented toward ergonomics, to grow, supported both by the need to rethink office space and by the need to set up work areas inside the home. We decided to continue the showroom expansion plan, despite the uncertain period in the middle of last year, in order to display a wider variety of products and solutions adapted to the current context and to the offices of the present and future, adds Horatiu Didea.

As remote work remains an option and even a norm for many companies in 2021, the office of the present is focused on flexibility more than ever. Meeting space can also become a cafe or relaxation area, just as a manager's office can become a meeting place for partners or project colleagues.

Office areas will be reduced by an average of around 30%, because they will serve a smaller number of employees coming to the office every day. From a design perspective, however, they will be airier, more comfortable and more oriented toward wellbeing, in other words, more ergonomic. Companies are already investing more in quality and in design that creates the right environment for socializing, connection, innovation and decision-making. Moreover, spaces must provide safe working conditions and support employee health, which is why high-quality materials that withstand repeated cleaning and sanitizing are more sought-after than ever, Horatiu Didea added.
