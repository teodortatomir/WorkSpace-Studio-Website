Press release / February 2024

Integrated projects and refurbishments kept Workspace Studio Group revenue at EUR 22.5 million in 2023.

Turnkey projects generated 60% of revenue, with approximately one in two projects being refurbishments of existing spaces.

Press releaseBack to press releases

In 2023, Workspace Studio Group, specialized in furniture and integrated high-quality office fit-out projects and a Certified Dealer of the MillerKnoll group in Romania, consolidated its position as a provider of premium office fit-out solutions, maintaining constant revenue of EUR 22.5 million, the same level reached in 2022. Integrated turnkey projects generated 60% of revenue, with approximately one in two projects being refurbishments of existing spaces.

Changes in the labor market, especially hybrid work, as well as the growing share of Generation Z employees, are driving the need to rethink and refurbish offices so they become destinations people enjoy using. Last year, half of Workspace Studio's projects were refurbishments with the objective of creating a flexible company space, relevant for people and for the company's identity. I estimate that next year the share of these projects will exceed 70%, of course also against the background of a lower volume of new office buildings, but especially because organizations need to align the design and functionality of their space with employee expectations and the new needs of companies, says Horatiu Didea, Managing Partner Workspace Studio.

Against the background of companies' need to offer employees attractive office spaces, with a strong emphasis on design and physical as well as social ergonomics, the orientation toward premium-quality solutions and multifunctionality continued in 2023. Varied and experiential design, space flexibility through sound-insulated partitioning solutions with superior technical characteristics, and the sustainability of solutions integrated into interior architecture are central aspects of all recent Workspace Studio fit-outs, directions that will continue in 2024.

2024 trends: refurbishments and design with impact. Spaces shrink, expectations rise.

As companies have felt the negative effects of remote work on organizational culture and people's commitment to the company, the role of the office has been redefined, becoming first and foremost a space for connecting with colleagues. Connection and collaboration take different forms depending on the organization, and the office must efficiently support several types of interaction: exchange of ideas, mentoring and onboarding and learning processes, teamwork, work meetings, strategy, or innovation. Modern office design must therefore have an impact on the quality of connection, acting as a catalyst for a superior employee workplace experience.

At the same time, the office remains a space for individual work for many employees for whom their own home or third spaces are not desirable options. Current offices must therefore also integrate focus or relaxation areas, offering acoustic comfort and on-demand privacy, as well as highly ergonomic workstations. The impact of design can thus be measured in the level of employee comfort and wellbeing, with space being a key element in any organization's employee experience strategy.

Another important trend taking shape this year is fit-outs that are easy to adapt to change, whether the changes can be made in minutes, hours, or days. Interest therefore remains high in multifunctional and modular solutions and spaces.

Any refurbishment project begins with the discussion about organizational culture and how the workspace can support it. Companies need spaces that strengthen community cohesion and where people connect emotionally. And, above all, spaces that remain relevant, attractive, and functional over time, but that can be changed very quickly to respond to new needs. Orientation toward employees is becoming more important than ever, and offices with a design that inspires and stimulates creativity are a source of competitive advantage and a necessity recognized by more and more organizations. In 2024, we estimate a focus on smaller spaces, greater cost pressure, but the need for quality and impact will remain, explains Horatiu Didea.

Investments of more than EUR 3 million in logistics and showroom

In the last quarter of 2023, Workspace Studio completed the investment of more than EUR 300,000 in expanding the showroom located on the 18th floor of Sky Tower Bucharest. In the expanded showroom, clients can explore furniture design brands such as Herman Miller, Knoll, HAY, NaughtOne, Geiger, Framey Acoustics, BuzziSpace, Renz, sustainable acoustic solutions such as those from Impact Acoustic, Maars partitioning systems, Mohawk flooring, as well as the latest trends in design and office fit-out solutions, with inspiration for modern, personalized, and innovative work areas.

Workspace Studio also invested more than EUR 3 million in a 6,000 sqm logistics space located in the western area of Bucharest. This allows the optimization of large fit-out projects that take several months, the preparation of furniture pieces and integrated solutions for turnkey projects, and faster delivery and customer service processes.

All the projects we have implemented in recent years have had serious time constraints. In other words, companies want a new office quickly, or a very fast refresh of the existing space. To increase efficiency and reduce waiting times for solutions integrated into fit-outs, last year we chose to invest in our own logistics unit where we store both furniture pieces from the brands we represent and regularly include in our fit-outs, as well as construction materials and architectural products needed in fit-out projects. In addition, our clients increasingly need flexible solutions, much faster logistics response, and increasingly agile customer service. Integrated control of logistics will allow us to respond at a high level to the constantly changing needs of our clients, says Horatiu Didea.

Ergonomic chairs, electric desks, acoustic booths, and soft seating remain the best-selling products

Herman Miller chairs are recognized worldwide for quality, ergonomics, and their 12-year warranty, and are appreciated for the technological innovations they propose. Among the most sought-after and best-selling products in 2023 were the Mirra 2 and Aeron chairs from Herman Miller, global ergonomic benchmarks that provide personalized adjustment according to individual physical characteristics.

The list of the best-selling products in 2023 is completed by adjustable desks, acoustic booths, and soft-seating products such as sofas and armchairs.

About Workspace Studio

Workspace Studio is a distributor of high-quality ergonomic office furniture and architectural products and the sole MillerKnoll Certified Dealer in Romania. The turnkey interior fit-out solutions proposed by Workspace Studio are personalized and integrated, with an emphasis on ergonomics, sustainability, and design centered on people and their activities and interactions.

One of the few players in the market focused on human-centric design, Workspace Studio differentiates itself through an extensive product portfolio that addresses ergonomics, sustainability, and comfort equally, contributing to productivity and quality of life.

Workspace Studio Group includes Workspace Studio and Allspace Interiors, specialized in ergonomic turnkey office fit-out solutions.
