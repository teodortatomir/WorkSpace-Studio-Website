Press release / July 2023

Workspace Studio reached EUR 8.5 million in revenue in the first six months of the year.

The company is focusing on sustainability, ergonomics, and human-centric design.

Press releaseBack to press releases

Workspace Studio Group, specialized in high-quality office design and fit-outs and a Certified Dealer of the MillerKnoll group in Romania, recorded revenue of EUR 8.5 million in the first six months of the year. Ergonomic, high-quality furniture, space flexibility through sound-insulated partitioning solutions, human-centric design, and the sustainability of office furniture and the materials used are the main characteristics of the projects completed in the first half of the year.

Specialized in high-quality office design and fit-outs with an emphasis on ergonomics, sustainability, and care for the environment, Workspace Studio has taken the initiative to distribute only manufacturers that hold sustainability-related certifications, from ISO and FSC to the use of certified raw materials. In acoustic solutions, Workspace Studio integrates into projects and proposes to clients exclusively solutions built from recycled, biodegradable materials or materials that are part of a circular economy system.

An important dimension of sustainability in design derives from multifunctionality. Using one piece for several purposes means more prudent resource management, lower total costs, more efficient use of space, and at the same time it stimulates creativity. For example, in acoustic solutions, most of the ideas proposed in Workspace Studio projects simultaneously reduce ambient noise and act as decorative elements or space-partitioning systems. In this way, the space is not crowded with a variety of products, but one piece is used for several purposes: lighting fixtures made from materials with an acoustic role, separating walls made from acoustic materials, says Horatiu Didea, Managing Partner Workspace Studio.

Pressure on creativity in design: surfaces are shrinking, the quality trend remains

In the first half of the year, the focus on increasing the level of quality, ergonomics, design, and multifunctionality of furniture pieces continued, while leased areas showed a contraction trend of 15-20%. As a result, the average value of fit-out projects was EUR 300,000, with an average investment of EUR 500-1,000 per sqm.

Cost pressure is being felt, generated by inflation, the possible recession anticipated by several companies, the war in Ukraine, global economic uncertainty, and uncertainty about the future of hybrid work. In 2022, budgets preserved from 2020-2021 were spent, but this year planned budgets saw more restrictions. Correlated with a slight reduction in spaces, pressure on creativity is increasingly high and a very intelligent mix of design, solutions, and products is needed to create a remarkable office, explains Horatiu Didea.

The average allocated budget, which takes into account the individual workstation and the related share of common areas, remains around EUR 2,000 per workstation. Integrating furniture solutions and pieces with superior ergonomics and design, alongside a fit-out centered on the individual and responding to the current needs of the modern office, remains an important objective in every project.

In 3 out of 10 projects, the sustainability of solutions integrated into interior architecture is a key requirement

The focus on sustainable solutions is an explicit trend in current office fit-out projects, especially in the context of European directives on corporate sustainability reporting (CSRD), which require companies operating in the European Union to disclose information and data related to environmental, social, and governance aspects.

Thus, in 3 out of 10 fit-out projects completed by Workspace Studio in the first half of the year, sustainability is a key requirement in the specifications.

The sustainability component has moved beyond the optional stage and has become essential. Organizations are already preparing for the implementation of the directives that will be transposed into law. But it is important for sustainability to be viewed from several perspectives, not only from the perspective of the materials used, but also from that of physical and moral durability, meaning the use of pieces with extended warranties, that do not go out of fashion and are created for the long term, says Horatiu Didea.

Herman Miller furniture and NaughtOne sofas and armchairs, with 12-year and 10-year warranties respectively, Maars demountable partitions, alongside furniture pieces from Knoll, sofas, armchairs, tables used for lounge and relaxation areas, and acoustic solutions made from 100% recycled materials are the most popular choices among companies in projects with a sustainability component.

About Workspace Studio

Workspace Studio is a distributor of high-quality ergonomic office furniture and architectural products and the sole MillerKnoll Certified Dealer in Romania. The turnkey interior design solutions proposed by Workspace Studio are personalized and integrated, with an emphasis on ergonomics, sustainability, and design centered on people and their activities and interactions.

One of the few players in the market focused on human-centric design, Workspace Studio differentiates itself through an extensive product portfolio that addresses ergonomics, sustainability, and comfort equally, contributing to productivity and quality of life.

Workspace Studio Group includes Workspace Studio and Allspace Interiors, specialized in ergonomic turnkey office fit-out solutions.
