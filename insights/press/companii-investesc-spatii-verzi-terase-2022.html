Press release / October 2022

Companies invest substantially in green spaces, terraces and generous social areas.

The average investment in office fit-out projects reached EUR 350,000, while revenue reached EUR 10 million in the first nine months.

Press releaseBack to press releases

Workspace Studio, a company specialized in high-quality office design and fit-outs and a MillerKnoll Certified Dealer in Romania since June, recorded revenue of EUR 10 million in the first nine months, up 50% compared with the same period last year.

The positive momentum is driven by companies' growing interest in investing in office refurbishments aligned with new labor market trends: quality, rethinking workstations and increasing spaces for socializing, collaboration and mentoring at the expense of individual areas.

Projects delivered by Workspace Studio in the first nine months focused on premium design, quality and ergonomic furniture, as well as creating comfortable spaces that support wellbeing and stimulate productivity, creativity and collaboration. Ergonomic furniture together with specialized or premium solutions for collaboration areas generated 75% of total sales.

After two years of changes and transformations, companies are now looking more than ever to give office spaces a new meaning. They want to turn them into pleasant, comfortable, useful, practical and ergonomic spaces. Employees want offices that look better than those at home and seek variety in both design and posture. From the first months of the year, the trend toward design and high quality became visible, says Horatiu Didea.

The value of major refurbishment projects doubled

In the context of economic pressure and hybrid work, average total surfaces decreased by around 15%. At the same time, spaces became airier, with average areas per employee almost 50% higher than before the pandemic. If two or three years ago individual work zones represented 65% of fit-out project surfaces, today the proportion favors collaboration and social areas at 55%.

In the first nine months of the year, the average budget, including both the individual workstation and its share of common areas, exceeded EUR 2,000 per workstation, up 10% compared with the same period last year.

Against the backdrop of the return to the office and growing interest in workplace quality, the number of refurbishment projects increased significantly. Workspace Studio delivered fit-out and refurbishment projects with an average value of EUR 350,000, up 15% compared with 2021.

If there is one good thing we keep post-pandemic, it may be the transformation of the office and of the way we work. The transformation of offices into more beautiful and more selective destinations is irreversible. Companies invest substantially in green spaces, terraces, collaboration areas, relaxation areas and generous cafeterias, adds Horatiu Didea.

During the analyzed period, the most sought-after product solutions were ergonomic chairs, acoustic architectural claddings and walls, acoustic booths, informal furniture for discussion areas, residential-style furniture and furniture for cafeteria areas.

Companies decided both to release budgets frozen over the last two years and to invest in the quality of workspaces to accelerate employees' return to the office. Regional crisis, inflation and the risk of recession affected budgets, but even so, investments in quality, sustainability and wellbeing increased significantly, Horatiu Didea added.

With its expanded range, Workspace Studio addresses the market interested in quality pieces across office, commercial and premium residential projects. In the first nine months, B2B projects generated 90% of total revenue, and Workspace Studio Group targets revenue of more than EUR 14 million this year, up over 40% compared with 2021.

About Workspace Studio

Workspace Studio distributes high-quality ergonomic office furniture and architectural products and is the sole MillerKnoll Certified Dealer in Romania. Its turnkey interior design solutions are personalized and integrated, with an emphasis on ergonomics, sustainability and human-centric design.

Workspace Studio Group, which includes Workspace Studio and Allspace Interiors, closed 2021 with revenue of EUR 10 million.
