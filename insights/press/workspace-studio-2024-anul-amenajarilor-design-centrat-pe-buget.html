Workspace Studio: 2024 is the year of budget-centric office fit-outs.

Investments in ergonomics and design have increased by approximately 20%.

Press releaseBack to press releases

Workspace Studio, specialized in furniture and integrated high-quality office fit-out projects and a Certified Dealer of the MillerKnoll group in Romania, notes this year the transition toward projects in which companies aim to maximize the value and quality obtained, starting from a fixed budget. Budget-centric office fit-outs reflect a growing trend among companies interested in superior solutions that support modern office design and employee activity.

This paradigm shift is driven mainly by companies' increasing need for predictability in procurement processes, but also by the need to offer employees a work environment that not only meets functional specifications, but also inspires them and supports their wellbeing, productivity, and need for socialization and cooperation.

In recent years, Workspace Studio has developed its capabilities to approach projects in this manner, which emphasizes delivered quality and alignment between the organization's vision and specific needs, time, and budget. The budget-centric approach ensures that the fit-out project meets the established objectives in the best possible way, taking into account time constraints and, above all, budget constraints.

Workspace Studio is a direct distributor of more than 150 international brands and collaborates directly with more than 200 local manufacturers, which ensures minimum B2B purchase prices for products covering the entire suite of fit-out needs: flooring, partitions, glass walls, acoustic solutions, furniture, and decorations. The portfolio includes premium brands such as Knoll and Herman Miller, as well as lower-priced solutions that still offer contract-grade warranty and quality for intensive use.

In addition, Workspace Studio's EUR 3.5 million investment in logistics, in its own warehouse, ensures a controllable end-to-end logistics chain and a high level of implementation accuracy in terms of time and cost.

The transition from a purely utilitarian approach, where minimum cost was decisive, to a strategy focused on maximizing the value that each euro invested can bring to employees and, implicitly, to the organization, has become especially visible after the pandemic. Offices can no longer remain spaces without identity and without comfort. To attract talent and cultivate an organizational culture based on innovation, collaboration, and exchange of ideas, attractive spaces are needed, spaces that serve the need for interaction, connection, or focus, at a very high standard of physical and social ergonomics. To create truly impactful design, aligned with the company's vision but also with budget restrictions, consulting, project management, and design are needed, with an emphasis on integrating design with the building infrastructure, therefore using the existing base efficiently, says Horatiu Didea, Managing Partner at Workspace Studio.

The trend is driven by a deeper understanding that investments in office design are not just expenses, but strategic investments in human capital. Companies are now looking for solutions that balance costs with long-term benefits, such as attracting and retaining talent, increasing employee satisfaction, encouraging office presence, supporting emotional and physical health, and stimulating collaboration.

The lowest price is no longer the best long-term option, nor the most financially efficient one, because investment in fitting out a modern office is now seen as an investment in employees and has become a process involving a multidisciplinary team - HR, Facility Manager, and middle management - that evaluates the project from multiple perspectives, looking at its overall impact on organizational culture, company brand, productivity, level of innovation, and employees' physical and psychological wellbeing, explains Horatiu Didea.

Budget-centric design in office fit-outs and refurbishments: smarter investments by companies in better solutions

For a complete office refurbishment, costs vary between EUR 600 and EUR 1,000 per square meter, and can reach EUR 1,500 if absolutely all indirect and associated costs are considered. A company with around 100 employees and an office of 1,000 square meters will allocate between EUR 600,000 and EUR 1.5 million for a refurbishment. Costs are cut in half if the organization starts a refurbishment project and invests only in furnishing the office and a refurbishment focused only on design and atmosphere elements.

The budget-centric approach ensures the highest quality of fit-out while respecting the financial constraint, which always exists. The advantage over an approach based on the lowest cost is that specialists in the organization can choose the best solution that respects the technical specifications and creates a truly attractive space. This vision allows the integration of ergonomic solutions, with design, quality, and a high level of sustainability, which can transform offices into true destinations for collaboration, exchange of ideas, mentoring, and innovation. Our proposals for such projects include multiple solutions, which are always modular, with fine cost accuracy. They provide a very clear picture of the entire project, and the impact of a change is reflected in real time in the budget and timeline. This preliminary study of needs, consulting, and offering with multiple design solutions are free and part of the procurement process, says Horatiu Didea.

In the first quarter of 2023, Workspace Studio Group completed projects worth EUR 5.5 million. The average value of integrated projects implemented by Workspace Studio between January and March was EUR 500,000.

Turnkey, but flexible solutions

The transition from utilitarian approaches, focused strictly on minimizing costs, to strategies that leverage each euro invested to maximize impact on employees aligns with the market's need for adaptability and sustainability.

Companies want spaces that evolve together with changes in the organization and offer possibilities for rapid and efficient adaptation to emerging requirements. The ability to easily adapt the space to change is a mandatory requirement in this year's projects, adds Horatiu Didea.

Companies need office spaces to be highly adaptable, so investments in refurbishment go first toward solutions that have a flexibility component. Acoustic booths, which are easy to dismantle and install in another space, mobile partitions, acoustic solutions, and modular furniture are basic pieces in modern offices. Alongside these, ergonomic chairs and height-adjustable electric desks are essential pieces for creating a workstation that supports employee health.

Each refurbishment project is initially guided by an analysis of organizational culture and the workspace's ability to amplify it. Companies are looking for spaces that strengthen community cohesion and facilitate emotional connections between employees. These spaces must remain relevant, attractive, and functional over the long term, while also allowing quick modifications to adapt to emerging requirements. Orientation toward employee needs becomes essential, and offices designed to inspire and stimulate creativity are a competitive advantage recognized by more and more companies. For 2024, the trend toward using more compact spaces continues, where cost pressure is higher, but quality requirements and design impact remain priorities, explains Horatiu Didea.

Studies initiated by MillerKnoll, the conglomerate of office furniture brands represented by Workspace Studio in Romania, show that from the perspective of interest in going to the office, employees fall into three categories. Approximately 20% come to the office because they work better in that space, including for focused and individual work. They need very good working conditions. Then there are those who go to the office to collaborate and socialize with colleagues, representing about 60% of all employees. The remaining 20% go to the office for internal visibility, meaning to be present for career development.

All of them need better ergonomic and design solutions than they had three or five years ago. This is a trend that has remained solid, and I see that the budgets companies allocate for ergonomic fit-outs have increased by approximately 20% compared with previous years. Interest in sustainable and inclusive design is also visibly growing, adds Horatiu Didea.
