Press release / February 2021

The new open office is airier, multifunctional, with acoustic booths and relaxation areas.

Workspace Studio outlines the trends shaping ergonomic office design.

Press releaseBack to press releases

Workspace Studio, a company specialized in ergonomic office design solutions and Herman Miller's only accredited partner in Romania, announces the trends taking shape in workplace design. Today's offices are adapted to a hybrid office-remote work system. Open spaces are becoming airier, with ergonomic and flexible workstations, acoustic booths provide privacy and on-demand separation, while multifunctional areas are growing as a share of total space.

Last year Workspace Studio delivered several dozen office fit-out and refurbishment projects for organizations that adopted a hybrid office-remote model. The company notes the directions that dominate the new philosophy of ergonomic workplace design in the post-pandemic era.

Open office becomes more open

After a complicated year for employers and employees alike, work has become more flexible both in terms of schedule and location. Offices are reinventing themselves as spaces for physical or virtual collaboration. They often become more exclusive, dedicated to the most valuable employees, those who innovate and create value.

Serving a smaller number of employees who come to the office every day, offices now integrate wider spaces, very good air ventilation and quality materials that allow constant sanitizing and cleaning. Open office spaces are becoming more open, airier and more flexible, says Horatiu Didea, Managing Partner Workspace Studio.

Smart acoustic booths

To facilitate teamwork, collaboration and idea exchange, the number of separated areas is increasing, not as rooms with walls, but as zones defined by decorative and furniture elements or acoustic booths. In 2020, acoustic booths generated sales for Workspace Studio that were twice as high as in the previous year.

This year, Workspace Studio brings to Romania Framery One, the first fully connected acoustic booth. Recently launched by the Finnish manufacturer Framery, a global leader in acoustic office solutions, Framery One includes integrated technology to support remote collaboration.

The Framery One booth is an ideal environment for video conferences. The distance between user, camera and screen is optimal, and exterior noise is completely removed, so remote collaboration is efficient and productive. These spaces, which ensure privacy and distancing as well as connection and presence, are becoming a constant in our projects this year, says Horatiu Didea.

Framery One is not only soundproofed, but also includes a high-resolution touch screen and an intuitive user interface. The user can control airflow and lighting, turning the booth into a fully connected personal workstation that allows separation inside open spaces.

Wellbeing and flexibility

Employee wellbeing has become a priority for people-oriented organizations. They are taking measures to maintain employees' physical and mental health so that they remain productive and engaged, regardless of where they work.

Just as workstation design is integrated into homes to adapt to work from home, kitchens, living areas and dining areas are harmonized into office design. The result is a flexible and warm place that makes employees feel at home. The psychological effect is increased engagement and performance, says Horatiu Didea.

Multifunctional areas for relaxation, connection, learning and idea exchange

The activities employees carry out at the office have changed fundamentally in recent years thanks to the increased mobility of work tools such as laptops, mobile phones and access to data. Individual processing work can be done from anywhere, while the office remains the destination for innovation, teamwork, collaboration, idea exchange and information sharing.

After months of distancing, employees return to the office with a desire to connect, but also with some hesitation around closeness. Offices therefore need more relaxation and informal socializing areas, airy cafeterias and spaces that can support varied activities. Multifunctional areas can exceed 50% of office surface.

The time of brainstorming sessions held in formal meeting rooms has passed. These interactions can take place one day in the cafeteria, another day standing at a work table separated by acoustic panels, and another day on Zoom. Flexibility is key and must be supported through multifunctionality, adds Horatiu Didea.
