Press release / Bucharest, November 2021

Mihaela Petrescu is Workspace Studio's new Business Development Director.

The fit-out budget per workstation increased by 30% compared with the pre-pandemic period.

With almost 10 years of experience in the office market, both in real estate consultancy and interior fit-out, Mihaela Petrescu takes over the role of Business Development Director at Workspace Studio, a company specialized in ergonomic office design solutions and Herman Miller's only accredited partner in Romania.

From this position, Mihaela facilitates interaction between tenants and landlords, drawing on her previous exposure so that the deep changes in the labor market can be assimilated quickly and efficiently, and local offices can develop to new standards.

Our market is marked by profound transformations, with the work paradigm changing completely in the context of accelerated adoption of hybrid work. Mihaela Petrescu has experience both in office fit-outs and in real estate consultancy, and our team's mission is to activate dialogue with the main market players and accelerate implementation of high-quality solutions, says Horatiu Didea, Managing Partner Workspace Studio.

The new office paradigm: a 360-degree user-centered approach

Studies show that hybrid work is preferred by almost 75% of Romanians who worked in offices before the pandemic. Local organizations are therefore adopting hybrid work systems for shares ranging from 20% to 100% of employees.

In this context, office fit-outs and refurbishments aim to adapt company space to this system, emphasizing social, collaboration and idea-exchange areas, as well as solutions that connect colleagues working from the office with those working remotely.

The share of common areas in Workspace Studio projects, including social and teamwork spaces, has risen to almost 50% of project value, compared with 20-25% before the pandemic. These are relaxed, intimate spaces, more precisely adapted to specific activities, while also remaining flexible and naturally integrated with individual work areas.

Most conference rooms and individual offices have disappeared, replaced by acoustic booths that accommodate up to four to six people, dedicated areas for individual work and multifunctional spaces for teamwork, mentoring or presentations.

The preferred hybrid concept now requires reorganizing the workplace to accommodate a safe environment for interaction, integrating technologies that make conference systems easy to use, and adding places for socializing and co-working, such as electric desks, phone booths, acoustic panels or landscape walls. Office and building design are analyzed in detail to ensure a superior working environment, says Mihaela Petrescu.

The fit-out budget per workstation increased by 30%

Alongside the market-level shift in approach, companies are investing in fit-outs and refurbishments to attract employees back to the office. In 2021, 90% of Workspace Studio's fit-out or refurbishment projects were small and medium-sized projects adapted for teams of 50-200 people.

These projects stood out through user-centered design, ergonomics and a superior quality of integrated furniture solutions that provide increased comfort for employees. Most projects covered spaces between 300 and 1,000 square meters, with total project budgets between EUR 100,000 and EUR 500,000.

Design now supports the distancing that many employees psychologically need when returning to the office after almost two years of remote work, while also enabling connection and collaboration, the main reasons people want to return to the office.

The average budget allocated per employee in Workspace Studio projects increased compared with 2019, the year before the pandemic. Organizations now invest on average more than EUR 2,500 per workstation.
